### Problem Statement
Netflix wants to better understand which movie they should produce next, including the most suitable actors and directors. We have a dataset containing movie budgets, box office performance, actors, directors and genres. Your task is to build an Excel dashboard that provides insights into this dataset. The dashboard should help identify: 
- The best performing actors
- The top movies based on box office metrics
- Director performance
- Genre trends
- Seasonal patterns in movie performance
- Any additional insights that can guide future production decisions.
The final dashboard should be clear, interactive and visually compelling, enabling Netflix to make data-driven decision.

### Data source
Movie data: The primary dataset used for this analysis is the “Movie Data Homework.xlsx” file, containing detailed information about each movie’s performance (box office and budget), actors, directors, etc. Find Original Data Source Here: [Movie Dataset Excel file](https://github.com/user-attachments/files/26643339/Movies_Data_Homework.xlsx)

### Recommendations 
1. Prioritize High-Performing Genres
Action and Sci-Fi consistently generate the highest box office revenue, making them the safest choices for future investment.
Family and Fantasy genres also show moderate but stable performance—ideal for diversifying Netflix Originals.
Focus future productions on Action and Sci-Fi, while exploring expansion in Family and Fantasy for broader audience appeal.
 
2. Invest in Actors With Proven Box Office Success 
The Top 5 actors by box office revenue include: Jennifer Lawrence, Matt Damon, Matthew McConaughey, Dylan O’Brien,Shailene Woodley
These actors demonstrate consistently high commercial performance. Prioritize them (or talent with similar profiles) for lead roles in major productions.
 
3. Collaborate With High-ROI Directors
Top-performing directors by revenue: Francis Lawrence, Ridley Scott, Christopher Nolan, Wes Ball, Stephen Chow
These directors have led films with strong financial returns and large audience appeal.
Build strategic partnerships with directors who have strong historical ROI, particularly Francis Lawrence and Ridley Scott, who lead the list.
 
4. Leverage High-ROI Movie Patterns 
Movies like The Purge, Lucy, Chronicle, and The Maze Runner show exceptionally high ROI, despite not being the top in revenue.
What this means: High-ROI movies often, Have lower budgets, Target specific fan bases, Use unique concepts or storytelling styles
Develop mid-budget, high-concept Sci-Fi or thriller projects, which historically yield strong ROI.
